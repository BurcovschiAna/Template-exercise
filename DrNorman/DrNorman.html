Here's time line of Dr. Norman Borlaug's life:

  • 1914 - born in Cresco, Iowa
  • 1933 - Leavs his family's farm to attend teh University of Minnesota thanks to a Depression era program known as the "National Youth Administration"
  • 1935 - Has to stop school and save up money. Works in the Civilian Conservation Corps, helping starving Americans " I saw how food changed them" he said. "All of this left cars on me"
  • 1937 - Finishes university and takes a job in the US Foresty Service
  • 1938 - Mareies wife of 69 years Margeret Gibson. Gets laid due to the budget cuts. Inspired by Elvin Charles Stakman, he returnes to school study unde Stakman, Who teaches him about breeding pest-resistent plants
  • 1941 - Tries to enroll in the military after the Pearl Harbor atack but is rejected. Instead the military asked his lab to work on watherproof glue, DDT to control malaria, disinfectants, and other applied science
  • 1942 - recived a PhD in Genetics and Plant Pathology
  • 1944 - Rejects a 100% salary increase from Dupont, leaves behind his pregnent wife, and flies to Mexico to head a new plant phatology program. Over the next 16 years, his team build 6000 different strains o diseases resistent wheat - including diferent variates for each major climat on Earth
  • 1945 - discover how t grow wheat twice each seson, doubling wheat yields
  • 1962 - Visits Delhi and brings his high-yieding strains of wheat to India subcontinent in time to mitigate mass starvation due to a rapidly expanding population.
  • 1970 - Reciver Nobel Peace Prize
  • 1983 - Helps seven African countries dramaticaly increase their maze and sorghum yields
  • 1984 - becomes a distingueshed profesor at Texas A&M University
  • 2005 - states "we willhave to double the world food supply by 2050" Argues geneticlly modified crops are the only way we can meet the demand, as we run out of arable land. Says that GM crops are not inheretly dangerouse because "we've been geneticly modiefied plants and animals for a long time. Long before we caled it science, people were selecting the best breeds"
  • 2009 - dies at age of 95
